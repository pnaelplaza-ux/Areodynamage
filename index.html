Aerodynamics — quick guide

Lift: Force perpendicular to flow created by pressure differences around the shape; in this simulation it appears as flow turning and upward/downward deflections near edges.

Drag: Force opposing motion caused by flow separation and frontal area; measured here as the "Drag index" which roughly reflects how much of the silhouette blocks the stream.

Turbulence & Gusts: Random and coherent perturbations that mix the flow — raising turbulence increases chaotic small-scale motion, gusts add larger time-varying pulses.

Vortex Effects: Swirl and lift generated near the trailing edges; controlling vortex strength adjusts how strongly flow wraps and spins behind the obstacle.

Tip: Import different shapes and use the sliders to explore how profile, size and orientation change lift/drag behavior visually.
